A funny story

In 2017 I decided to pursue a lifelong dream: travel the world. This was truly something I had fantasized about since I was a young child, especially whenever I was heading back home after a vacation abroad, in tears once again.

While many peers around me were mirthfully traveling, I too began looking for a willing victim to join me and explore the world. Many eagerly agreed to go on an adventure of a lifetime but in the end nobody really committed, you know how it goes… After all, it is a big step to leave the comfort of your home to go and wander around with nothing more than what you can carry in your backpack.

At this point in my life, however, the fear of never having done a major trip and regretting it was greater than the fear of leaving alone. So, here there it was: I was going to travel the world alone. A-L-O-N-E. I quit my job I sincerely loved, I gave up my apartment with a gorgeous garden that was rediciulmously underpriced. I could do this, I had always been an independent, resourceful person. And I was an introverted person anyway, maybe I’d never ever feel lonely and even relish my time being alone.

The very first day of my grandiose trip starting in New Zealand, I discovered that the airline I was flying with required proof of me also exiting the country. Since my plan was to have no plan, I didn’t book any tickets in advance. Without it, however, I was not allowed on the plane. I was left with no other choice but to book a random exiting ticket from New Zealand right there and then. No worries, disaster averted.

My ordeal was not over yet. There were no available seats at the gate where my plane was due to depart, so I decided to and wait one gate over. In all my excitement, thought, I completely forgot this and only realized when I happened to take off my headphones and could hear my name over the intercom for the last call to New Zealand. I barely caught my first flight on time – look at me being all independent!

      

TL;DR

When I left to go travel the world alone, I barely caught my very first flight because I forgot I wasn’t waiting at the correct gate.
